About:
Arnold Alois Schwarzenegger (/ˈʃwɔːrtsənɛɡər/ SHWORT-sə-neg-ər, Austrian German: [ˈarnɔlt ˈaːlɔʏs ˈʃvartsn̩ˌɛɡɐ]; born July 30, 1947) is an Austrian-American actor, businessman, former politician, and professional bodybuilder, known for his roles in high-profile action films. He served as the 38th governor of California from 2003 to 2011.

More Info :
Schwarzenegger began lifting weights at age 15, won the Mr. Universe title at 20, and subsequently claimed the Mr. Olympia title seven times. He is tied with Phil Heath for the second-most Mr. Olympia wins, behind Ronnie Coleman and Lee Haney, who share the record with eight wins each. Nicknamed the "Austrian Oak" during his bodybuilding career, he is regarded as one of the greatest bodybuilders of all time. Schwarzenegger has authored books and articles on bodybuilding, including Arnold: The Education of a Bodybuilder (1977) and The New Encyclopedia of Modern Bodybuilding (1998). The Arnold Sports Festival, the second-most prestigious bodybuilding event after Mr. Olympia, is named in his honor. His appearance in the documentary Pumping Iron (1977) helped launch his film career.
